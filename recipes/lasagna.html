Lasagna

Classic Italian comfort food with layers of pasta, meat sauce, and melted cheese

Description

This classic Italian lasagna recipe features layers of tender pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy b√©chamel, and melted cheese. It's a comforting dish that's perfect for family dinners or special occasions.

The recipe combines traditional techniques with accessible ingredients to create a lasagna that's both authentic and approachable for home cooks.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C).
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package directions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  4. Add diced onion and minced garlic to the skillet. Cook until onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with the egg until well combined.
  7. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  8. Place 4 lasagna noodles over the sauce, slightly overlapping.
  9. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then sprinkle with 1/3 of the mozzarella cheese.
  10. Repeat layers: meat sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella.
  11. Top with remaining noodles, meat sauce, and mozzarella cheese. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese on top.
  12. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  13. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until cheese is bubbly and golden.
  14. Let stand for 10 minutes before serving.

Cooking Tips

  • Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes after baking for easier slicing
  • Use fresh herbs when available for enhanced flavor
  • Don't overcook the noodles - they should be al dente
  • Layer the ingredients evenly for consistent cooking
  • Cover with foil for the first 25 minutes to prevent drying out
